ASAP Constitution

Article I: Organization Name

The name of the organization shall be ASAP (Asian Student Arts Project).

Article II: Purpose

The purpose of ASAP is to provide the network and resources for Asian and Asian-American students across multiple disciplines including theater, film, dance, music, comedy, writing, entertainment, media, and more to make art and create a community for these artists with a particular focus on dramatic and visual arts.

ASAP aims to celebrate the community, experience, culture, and history of Asian students at Harvard with events such as group discussions, movie screenings, workshops, open-mic nights, group outings, panels, and large- and small-scale performances and theatrical productions.

ASAP will aim to produce at least one arts project that concerns Asian or Asian-American culture, experiences, history, and/or arts each semester.

Article III: Membership

Membership in this organization shall be open to all students in good standing currently enrolled in Harvard College, regardless of race, creed, color, sex, gender identity, sexual orientation, or physical disability.

Members must be students of Harvard University, but membership is not limited to undergraduates.

Membership shall be retained until the student servers relations with the University whether by graduation or otherwise. Membership will be retained during the vacation and recesses of the university.

Any affiliate of Harvard University may participate in activities or programs (alumni, wider community, etc.)

Problematic behavior within the community may be addressed by members of the board on a case-by-case analysis.

Article IV: Officers

All officers shall be registered undergraduates of Harvard College

The officers of this group shall consist of the President or Co-Presidents, Vice President, Treasurer, Secretary, Outreach Chair, and First-Year Representatives.

  • The President(s) shall be the chief executive officer of the group and shall have general supervision and control of its activities and programs. The President will schedule and manage board meetings, oversee club activities and ensure that they are in line with ASAPâ€™s vision, and be the main face and spokesperson of the club.
  • The Vice President shall assist the President and assume their duties when the President or any other position is unable to serve.
  • The Treasurer shall have general charge of the financial affairs of the group. They shall keep accurate records of these affairs.
  • The Outreach Chair shall promote publicity, diversity within the club with regards to pan-Asian representation and multidisciplinary art forms, and greater communication to other clubs.
  • The Secretary shall keep a record of all meetings of the group and of all activities and programs. They will handle all communication and formal correspondence between members and officers of the group.
  • The First-Year Representatives shall participate in all board meetings and be in charge of attracting interest across campus. In particular, this role will ensure that incoming students have an opportunity to get involved and gain experience for future leadership. They will also be in charge of executing Open Mic Night events.

Article V: Elections

Officers shall be elected annually by the members of the group. Elections shall be held during the month of November.

The term of office shall start during the beginning of spring semester. The term of office for First-Year Representatives shall start immediately after they have been elected.

To ensure a smooth transition of office, incoming officers elected in November will shadow outgoing officers until the beginning of spring semester, when their term of office begins (with the exception of the founding year).

Potential officers must declare their candidacy up until election day, and walk-on candidates may also be considered on a case-by-case basis.

Officers may run individually for each position. Candidates for the position of President or Vice-President can run in pairs.

Elections will consist of speeches given by potential officers. An immediate blind ballot will be held after all of the speeches for each position have finished.

Election results for each position will be announced immediately following each position ballot.

If not chosen for a position, the nominee(s) will be eligible to run for subsequent positions. If chosen, however, the nominee(s) will be ineligible to run for other positions.

If an officer is unable to complete their term, an impromptu election will be held to replace them, excluding current officers (with the exception of the President, who will be replaced by the Vice President when they are unable to complete their duties as President).

First-Year Representatives will be given the primary consideration for the vacant position.

In the case of a tie, a discussion without the nominees will be held, followed by a re-vote.

Article V: Meetings

Meetings of the groups shall be held on a weekly basis for the purpose of determining policy, activities, programs, and other business. Special meetings of the group may be called by the President, Vice President, the Secretary, or any five members of the group.

A simple majority of the group shall constitute a quorum.

The President shall chair all meetings. In the President's absense, the Vice President shall chair.

Notice of regular and special meetings shall be communicated to all members of the group by the Secretary.

All members shall have one vote with respect to any resolutions put forward during a meeting.

Attendance will be taken at each meeting. After three unexcused absenses, the board member in question's position will be reconsidered by the board.

A proposal for events, shows, or other ideas may be submitted by anyone via email or in-person to be reviewed during regular meetings. Time will be set aside during meetings for suggestions, discussions, and submissions. A majority vote will determine the acceptance of proposals.

Board members that vote "yes" are required to attend and/or be involved in the organization of these events that they have approved.

Article VII: Amendments

The constitution may be amended by a two-thirds majority vote of the group.

Any member of the group may propose an amendment. Proposals shall be filed in writing with the Secretary prior to the meeting so that the group may debate the proposal.
